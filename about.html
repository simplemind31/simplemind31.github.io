Why Computer Science

I have always enjoyed solving puzzles and logical problems because they feel like a kind of mental sport. This interest first led me to Math Olympiads, where I learned how to think creatively and stay patient when a problem didn't make sense at first.

In 9th grade, I discovered Informatics, and working with computers became another way for me to challenge myself. I noticed that many of the strategies I used in math also helped me in programming, which motivated me to explore Informatics competitions more seriously.

Choosing Computer Science as my first-choice major is simply the next step in something I already love. Programming allows ideas to become real systems that can impact people's lives. Beyond competitions, I want to contribute to a world that becomes more digital every year.

For me, Computer Science represents curiosity, creativity, and meaningful problem solving. It is the path where I can continue challenging myself while building technology that shapes the future.
